Key concepts for trading system architecture

HFT
High-Frequency Trading
Trading strategy that executes thousands/millions of orders per second, competing on microseconds.
  • Latency measured in microseconds (Œºs) or nanoseconds
  • Servers co-located at exchange (same building)
  • Makes tiny profit per trade, but millions of trades
  • Custom hardware (FPGAs), kernel bypass networking
  • Every microsecond of overhead = money lost
Citadel Securities Virtu Jump Trading Tower Research
HA
High Availability
System design ensuring your service stays online even when components fail.
  • Measured in "nines": 99.9% = 8.7 hours downtime/year
  • 99.99% (4 nines) = 52 minutes downtime/year
  • Achieved through redundancy (multiple instances)
  • Automatic failover when primary fails
  • Goal: never stop trading due to single component failure

üéØ When to Use Each
Use Active-Passive When:
  • You're doing HFT (every microsecond matters)
  • Redis ~1ms overhead is unacceptable
  • You have other redundancy (multiple strategies/exchanges)
  • Brief failover gaps are acceptable
  • Simplicity is valued
Use Active-Active + Redis When:
  • You're doing standard MM (milliseconds are fine)
  • ~1ms overhead is acceptable
  • Zero downtime is critical
  • You want to use all your hardware
  • Failover gaps = missed money
üí° Key Insight
"The choice depends on where you are on the latency spectrum. For HFT where you're competing in microseconds, Active-Passive makes sense ‚Äî you can't afford the ~1ms Redis overhead on every order. The 2-5 second failover gap is acceptable because you're also diversified across strategies and exchanges.

For standard market making like Acheron (millisecond-level), I'd recommend Active-Active with Redis coordination. The ~1ms overhead is noise when your orders take 5-50ms to reach the exchange anyway. And the instant failover means you never miss an opportunity or leave positions unmanaged.

The real question is: is 1ms overhead worth zero-downtime failover? For most crypto MM, the answer is yes."
